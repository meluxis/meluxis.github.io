Uni-DAD studies how to adapt and distill diffusion models in a unified framework for few-step, few-shot image generation.

The project focuses on making diffusion models more efficient and more adaptable to limited target-domain data. It connects my broader research interests in diffusion models, optimization, efficient generative modeling, and production-oriented generative AI.

The work is motivated by a practical question: how can we adapt large diffusion models to new domains while also making them fast enough for few-step generation? Uni-DAD addresses both objectives in a single training framework, making it especially relevant for efficient and production-minded generative systems.
