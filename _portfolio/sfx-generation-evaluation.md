This project focuses on evaluating generative audio systems for sound effects production.

Industrial sound design requires models that do more than generate realistic audio. They must preserve the perceptual identity of a reference, support controllable variation, remain efficient for iterative workflows, and adapt to heterogeneous production needs.

In this work, I developed a production-oriented evaluation framework for reference-guided SFX variation. The framework defines practical requirements and compares heterogeneous audio generation and editing methods under a shared production objective.

The broader goal is to evaluate generative methods the way production teams actually experience them: not only through fidelity, but also through identity preservation, diversity, controllability, temporal alignment, robustness, and efficiency.
