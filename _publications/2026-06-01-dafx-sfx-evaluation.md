This work proposes a production-oriented evaluation framework for reference-guided sound effects generation.

The framework studies practical requirements for SFX variation, including fidelity, identity preservation, diversity without drift, temporal alignment, energy control, controllability, targeted modification, robustness, and efficiency. It compares heterogeneous audio generation and editing methods under a shared production objective, while also preserving method-specific analyses such as SFX morphing, inpainting, temporal alignment, and targeted editing.
